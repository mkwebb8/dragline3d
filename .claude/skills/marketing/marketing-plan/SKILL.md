# Marketing Plan

Generate a comprehensive 12-month marketing strategy. The output: a single, executable document — the kind a fractional CMO would present to founders. Customized to actual budget, team size, and stage. Not generic.

## Three-Phase Workflow

**Phase 1 — INIT**: Research client data, conduct intake, score against 17-section current-state rubric, document in `research.md`.

**Phase 2 — REVIEW**: Present each of 13 plan sections interactively. Approve, adjust, or expand each before moving forward. Resumable if interrupted.

**Phase 3 — FINALIZE**: Compile sections into final deliverable; verify cross-references and brand consistency.

## The 13 Sections

1. Executive summary
2. Strategic frame (positioning and objectives)
3. Current state (scored against rubric)
4. Acquisition strategy
5. Activation strategy
6. Retention strategy
7. Referral strategy
8. Revenue strategy
9. 90-day roadmap (immediate priorities)
10. 12-month outlook (quarterly milestones)
11. Marketing ops stack (tools and integrations)
12. Tactical idea bank
13. Measurement & RACI

## What Gets Customized

Budget breakdown, unit economics (ARPC, retention rate, LTV), team composition, current channels and status, acknowledged past work, growth phase, future funding milestones, and required skills per move.

## Key Differentiators

- **AARRR-tagged**: Every tactic names which funnel stage it serves
- **Honest constraints**: Avoids recommending work the team can't execute
- **Funding-stage unlocks**: Explains what changes when budget closes

## When NOT to Use

Single-channel tactics or idea generation without strategic commitment — use marketing-ideas instead.

## Integration

All other marketing skills contribute to specific plan sections.
