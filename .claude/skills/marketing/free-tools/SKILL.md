# Free Tools (Engineering as Marketing)

Build useful resources you give away to attract customers. The best free tools solve a real problem adjacent to your product — genuinely useful without purchasing, but naturally leading to what you sell.

## Tool Types

| Type | Example | SEO Potential | Lead Potential |
|------|---------|---------------|----------------|
| Calculator | ROI calculator | Medium | High |
| Generator | Bio generator, email subject generator | High | Medium |
| Analyzer | SEO analyzer, readability checker | High | High |
| Converter | File converter, unit converter | High | Low |
| Grader | Website grader, resume grader | Medium | High |
| Template | Resume builder, invoice generator | High | Medium |

## Feasibility Scorecard

Rate 1–5:
- Adjacent to core product (high = natural upsell)
- Specific audience problem it solves
- Search volume for the use case
- Build complexity (lower = better for MVP)
- Defensibility (can competitors copy it easily?)

## Lead Capture Strategy

**Gate completely**: Require email to see results — higher friction but captures leads
**Gate partially**: Show some results; gate the full report — balances reach and capture
**Don't gate**: Maximize reach and SEO — use for top-of-funnel awareness tools

## SEO Positioning

Target the keyword "[tool type] for [use case]" or "[adjective] [tool name] tool." Free tools rank for high-volume keywords that content can't rank for.

## Build vs. Buy vs. Embed

- **Custom build**: Full control; requires engineering; best for defensible tools
- **No-code** (Webflow, Glide): Fast; limited complexity; good for MVPs
- **Embed** (third-party tools with white-labeling): Fastest; least differentiated
