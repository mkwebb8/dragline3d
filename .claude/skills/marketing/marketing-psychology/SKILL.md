# Marketing Psychology & Mental Models

Apply psychological principles to marketing decisions. Behavior requires: Capability, Opportunity, Motivation (COM-B Model). When facing low conversions, check activation energy, friction, and whether customers can actually complete the desired action.

## Four Categories

### 1. Foundational Thinking Models
- **First Principles**: What is actually true? Strip away assumptions.
- **Jobs to Be Done**: What job is the customer hiring your product to do?
- **Inversion**: What would make this fail? Avoid that.
- **Theory of Constraints**: What is the single bottleneck? Fix that first before optimizing elsewhere.

### 2. Understanding Buyers (Cognitive Biases)
- **Confirmation Bias**: People seek information confirming what they already believe — meet them where they are
- **Availability Heuristic**: Recent, vivid examples feel more likely — use specific stories, not statistics alone
- **Loss Aversion**: Losses feel ~2x more painful than equivalent gains — frame around what they'll lose by not acting
- **Status Quo Bias**: Default options win — make your product the obvious default

### 3. Influencing Behavior (Ethical Persuasion)
- **Reciprocity**: Give value first (free tools, content, templates) — creates obligation to return
- **Scarcity**: Limited availability increases perceived value — must be real, not manufactured
- **Authority**: Credentials, publications, and expert endorsements increase trust
- **Social Proof**: Specific numbers and named customers outperform generic claims

### 4. Design & Delivery
- **BJ Fogg's Behavior Model**: Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Prompt. Increase all three.
- **Hick's Law**: More choices = slower decisions = more abandonment. Reduce options.
- **Anchoring Effect**: First number seen sets the reference point for all subsequent numbers

## For Pricing Specifically

- Charm pricing ($99 < $100) — left-digit dominance
- Anchoring — show expensive option first
- Framing: "Save $200/year" outperforms "Get 2 months free" even at same value
