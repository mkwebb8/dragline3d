# Popup CRO

Create and optimize popups, modals, overlays, and banners. Too early = annoying interruption; too late = missed opportunity.

## Six Trigger Strategies

1. **Time-based**: After X seconds on page (30–60 seconds is common)
2. **Scroll-based**: After user scrolls past X% of page (50–70%)
3. **Exit-intent**: Mouse moves toward browser close (captures abandoning visitors)
4. **Click-triggered**: User clicks a button or link (highest intent)
5. **Page count**: After visiting X pages in a session
6. **Behavior-based**: After completing a specific action

## Popup Types

- **Email capture**: Core offer + single field (just email)
- **Lead magnet**: Specific asset in exchange for email
- **Discount**: Percentage or dollar off for first purchase
- **Exit-intent**: Last chance offer when leaving
- **Announcement**: No capture; inform about news or features
- **Slide-in**: Less intrusive; appears from corner

## Copy Formula

**Headline**: Specific benefit, not generic "Sign up for our newsletter"
**Subhead**: What they get + when (immediately)
**CTA**: Action word + outcome ("Get the checklist" not "Submit")
**Dismiss**: Make it obvious. "No thanks, I prefer [ironic cost]" is manipulative — use a plain X.

## Rules

- Frequency cap: Once per session minimum; once per 14–30 days maximum
- Never show on checkout or payment pages
- Never block scroll on mobile
- Provide visible close button immediately (GDPR + UX)

## Measurement Benchmarks

- Email popup conversion: 2–5% of visitors
- Exit-intent: 3–7% of abandoning visitors
- Discount popup: 5–15% (intent is higher)

## Integration

- lead-magnets: popup to deliver lead magnet
- emails: delivery sequence after popup capture
- ab-testing: testing popup timing, copy, and offers
- cro: popups as part of broader conversion optimization
