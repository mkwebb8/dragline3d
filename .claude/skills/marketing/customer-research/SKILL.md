# Customer Research

Uncover what customers actually think, feel, and struggle with. Real customer language beats polished copy every time.

## Two Modes

### Mode 1: Analyzing Existing Research
If you have customer interviews, survey results, support tickets, reviews, or win/loss data, extract:
- Pains and problems (exact language)
- Trigger moments (what caused them to seek a solution)
- Desired outcomes (what they're actually trying to achieve)
- Objections and concerns
- How they describe the problem before knowing your solution

### Mode 2: Finding Research Online
Mine where customers speak unfiltered:
- Reddit communities where your audience spends time
- G2 / Capterra / App Store reviews (competitor reviews especially)
- LinkedIn comment sections
- Industry forums and Slack communities
- YouTube comment sections on relevant videos

## Outputs

- **Theme synthesis**: Clustered pain points and language patterns
- **Persona documents**: Grounded in real data, not assumptions
- **Quote bank**: Verbatim customer language for copywriting
- **Research gap report**: What you know vs. what you need to find out

## Key Questions to Answer

1. What problem were they trying to solve when they found us?
2. What did they try before?
3. Why did alternatives fail?
4. What does success look like for them?
5. What almost stopped them from buying?

## Integration

- product-marketing: research feeds directly into positioning
- copywriting: customer language becomes headline and copy material
- content-strategy: pain points become content topics
- cold-email: trigger events and pain language for outreach
