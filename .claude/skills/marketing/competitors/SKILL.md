# Competitor & Alternative Pages

Build comparison pages that convert evaluators. Depth over checklists — explain why differences matter through use cases, not just feature tables.

## Four Page Formats

1. **"[Competitor] alternative"** — User wants to switch; high purchase intent
2. **"[Competitor] alternatives"** — Research phase; show you understand the category
3. **"You vs. competitor"** — Direct comparison; own your positioning
4. **"Competitor vs. competitor"** — Neutral comparison; introduces your product without being a partisan

## Core Principles

"Acknowledge competitor strengths. Be accurate about your limitations. Don't misrepresent competitor features."

Honesty builds evaluator trust. Reviewers fact-check claims on G2 and Capterra — inaccuracies destroy credibility.

## Required Page Elements

- **TL;DR** (2–3 sentences): Key differences in plain language
- **Comparison table**: Feature parity and differences
- **Paragraph explanations**: Why the differences matter in practice
- **Use case positioning**: Who each product serves best (and when to choose the competitor)
- **Migration support**: How easy is it to switch from them?
- **Customer evidence**: Quotes from customers who switched

## Research Foundation

Before building pages, gather:
- Product specs and current pricing for each competitor
- G2/Capterra review themes (filter by competitor)
- Customer switching stories from your sales team
- Current competitive positioning language from their site

Maintain a centralized competitor data document. Update it quarterly.
