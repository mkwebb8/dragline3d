# Cold Email

Write cold emails that sound human and get replies. The standard: write like a smart colleague who noticed something relevant — not a sales machine. If removing the personalization would make the email feel generic, it's not personalized enough.

## Core Structures

**Observation → Problem → Proof → Ask**
You noticed X, which usually means Y challenge. Here's proof we've solved it. One low-friction ask.

**Trigger → Insight → Ask**
Relevant trigger event creates a specific problem. We've solved it. One ask.

## What Works

**Subject lines**: 2–4 words, lowercase, internal-sounding. "reply rates" not "Amazing Growth Opportunity!"

**Body**: Lead with the reader's world. One clear, low-friction ask (15-minute call, not 30). Specific proof (one result, not a feature list).

**Follow-ups**: Add a new angle each time. Never "just checking in." 3–5 touch sequence, 3–5 days apart.

## What Kills Reply Rates

- "I hope this email finds you well"
- Jargon: "leverage," "synergy," "circle back"
- Feature dumps instead of outcome proof
- Multiple links (triggers spam filters)
- Asking for 30-minute calls on first touch
- Subject lines with capital letters or exclamation points

## The Gut Check

Would you reply to this? Does it sound human when read aloud?

Every sentence must earn its place. Cold email is ruthlessly short.

## Integration

- prospecting: building the list before writing the emails
- copywriting: longer-form copy principles applied to email
- sales-enablement: follow-up assets after email opens a conversation
