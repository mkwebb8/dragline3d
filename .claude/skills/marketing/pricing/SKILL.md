# Pricing Strategy

Pricing decisions, packaging, and monetization. Align price with customer perceived value, not cost-plus math.

## Core Areas

- **Value-based pricing**: What is the customer's alternative? What outcome do you deliver?
- **Value metrics**: What to charge for — per-seat, per-usage, flat fee, outcome-based
- **Tier structure**: Good-Better-Best frameworks — feature gating and upsell architecture
- **Pricing research**: Van Westendorp (too cheap/cheap/expensive/too expensive) and willingness-to-pay studies
- **Price increases**: Strategies for raising prices without churn spikes
- **Pricing pages**: Best practices and psychology principles

## Required Inputs

1. Business context — product type, target market, GTM approach
2. Current state — existing pricing, conversion rates, ARPU, churn
3. Value proposition — primary value delivered, competitive alternatives
4. Goals — optimizing for growth, revenue, or profitability

## Good-Better-Best Framework

**Good** (entry): Solves the core problem. Low friction to start.
**Better** (mid): Adds features that serious users need. Your real target.
**Best** (top): Full capabilities + services. Anchors perceived value of Better.

Price mid tier at ~3–5x entry; price top tier at ~2–3x mid.

## Psychology Principles

- Charm pricing ($99 vs $100) — left-digit dominance
- Anchoring — show most expensive option first
- Decoy pricing — make your target tier look best by comparison
- Annual discount — 2 months free; improves retention and cash flow
