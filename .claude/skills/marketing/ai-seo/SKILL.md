# AI SEO

Optimize for AI citation and discoverability. Traditional SEO gets you ranked; AI SEO gets you cited. A well-structured page can earn citations even from lower search rankings if it's extractable and authoritative.

## Three Optimization Pillars

### 1. Structure — Make Content Extractable
- Definition blocks, comparison tables, FAQs, step-by-step sections
- Key answer passages: 40–60 words (extractable chunk size)
- Semantic HTML and logical heading hierarchies
- Machine-readable files: `/pricing.md`, `/llms.txt`

### 2. Authority — Build Citation-Worthiness
- Cite authoritative sources (+40% AI visibility)
- Include statistics with sources (+37%)
- Add expert quotes (+30%)
- Demonstrate E-E-A-T signals and subject expertise

### 3. Presence — Appear Where AI Looks
- Wikipedia, Reddit, review platforms (G2, Capterra), industry publications, YouTube
- Third-party citations often outweigh your own domain
- High-DR directory listings are heavily cited by AI engines

## Critical Actions

- Verify AI bots aren't blocked in robots.txt: GPTBot, PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot, Google-Extended
- Add structured data for articles, how-tos, FAQs, products
- Maintain freshness signals and author attribution
- Make pricing transparent and machine-readable (no JavaScript walls)
- Monitor AI citations monthly (Otterly, Peec AI, ZipTie)

## What Kills AI Visibility

Keyword stuffing, gated resources, outdated information, JavaScript-hidden pricing, separate "AI content" strategy.

## Google's Stance

"Best practices for SEO continue to be relevant for AI features." No special markup needed — write for humans first; structure for machines second.
