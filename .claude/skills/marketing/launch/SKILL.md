# Launch Strategy

Launching isn't a single event — it's an ongoing process of building momentum through strategic use of multiple channel types. Use the ORB Framework.

## ORB Framework

**Owned Channels** (email, blog, community, website): Long-term compound value and direct audience relationships. Build these first.

**Rented Channels** (social media, app stores, marketplaces): Algorithm-dependent visibility. Use to drive traffic back to owned assets.

**Borrowed Channels** (guest content, collaborations, influencer partnerships, PR): Credibility through established audiences. Requires relationship-building beforehand.

## Five-Phase Approach

1. **Internal Launch**: Test with friendly early users. Find critical bugs and UX failures.
2. **Alpha Launch**: External validation with controlled access (invite-only).
3. **Beta Launch**: Scale testing, generate buzz, start building waitlist.
4. **Early Access**: Validate at broader scale, convert early adopters into advocates.
5. **Full Launch**: Open self-serve signups, charge, go wide.

## Product Hunt Strategy

**Before**: Build relationships with potential hunters, get upvotes committed in advance.
**Day of**: Optimize visuals and messaging, engage in real-time in comments, drive traffic from own channels.
**After**: Convert Product Hunt traffic into owned relationships (email list, community).

## Post-Launch Focus

- Automated onboarding sequences for new signups
- Feature comparison content for SEO
- Interactive demos for self-serve evaluation
- Consistent momentum through regular update posts ("What we shipped this week")

## Integration

- emails: launch announcement and drip sequences
- social: channel-specific launch content
- co-marketing: borrowed channel amplification
- referrals: early adopter referral programs
- pr: media outreach timing
