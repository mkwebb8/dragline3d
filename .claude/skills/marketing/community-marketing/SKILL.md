# Community Marketing

Build communities around shared identity and member value — not just the product. Communities thrive when members get value from each other, not just from you.

## Community Flywheel

Members get value → they engage → engagement attracts more members → more members create more value → repeat.

Break the flywheel at any step and growth stalls.

## Platform Selection

| Platform | Best For |
|----------|----------|
| Discord | Gaming, dev, async chat |
| Slack | B2B professional communities |
| Circle | Cohort-based, structured content |
| Forum (Discourse) | Long-form, searchable knowledge |
| Facebook Groups | Consumer, broad audience |

## Member Journey Design

1. **Discovery**: How do new members find the community?
2. **Onboarding**: What's their first impression and first action?
3. **Activation**: When do they first get value from another member?
4. **Engagement**: What brings them back regularly?
5. **Contribution**: When do they start giving, not just taking?
6. **Leadership**: Who becomes an ambassador or moderator?

## Recurring Rituals

The engine of engagement. Examples: weekly check-in threads, monthly showcases, AMAs, member spotlights. Rituals create predictable participation.

## Ambassador Programs

Identify top contributors. Offer: early access, special badge, direct line to the team, invitations to contribute to product decisions. Never offer just discounts — that attracts discount seekers.

## Health Metrics

- Weekly Active Members (WAM) — not total members
- Post-to-member ratio
- Response rate to new member posts (target: >80% get a reply within 24 hours)
- Net Promoter Score among active members
