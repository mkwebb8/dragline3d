# Copy Editing

Improve existing copy through seven sequential sweeps, one dimension at a time. Good copy editing enhances rather than rewrites.

## The Seven Sweeps

1. **Clarity** — Can readers understand the message immediately?
2. **Voice and Tone** — Is brand personality consistent throughout?
3. **So What** — Does every claim answer why readers should care?
4. **Prove It** — Do claims have supporting evidence?
5. **Specificity** — Replace vague language with concrete details and numbers
6. **Heightened Emotion** — Add emotional resonance where it's missing
7. **Zero Risk** — Remove every barrier to taking action

## Additional Tools

**Expert Panel Scoring**: For high-stakes copy, score across 3–5 personas. Target 7+ per persona, 8+ average.

**Quick-Pass Checks**: Word, sentence, and paragraph-level edits for faster turnarounds.

**Content Refresh**: Updating outdated pages — check for stale stats, old competitor references, and expired offers.

## Key Principle

Check for product marketing context first to align edits with established voice and customer language. The iterative approach respects existing work while systematically improving results.

## Integration

- copywriting: writing new copy (vs. editing existing)
- product-marketing: voice, tone, and customer language reference
- cro: conversion-focused edits after copy quality is solid
