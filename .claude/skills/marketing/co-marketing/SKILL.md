# Co-Marketing

Identify partners and execute joint campaigns. Seek partners who share your buyer persona but solve adjacent problems in the workflow — not direct competitors.

## Finding Partners

**Start with your customers**: What tools do they use alongside yours? Those companies share your audience.

**Tools**: Crossbeam (account overlap), G2 Neighbor Analysis, PartnerStack marketplace

**Scoring criteria** (rate 1–5):
- Audience overlap and size
- Brand alignment
- Non-competitive relationship
- Reciprocal promotional capacity
- Ease of relationship development

## Campaign Types

| Type | Effort | Lead Capture | Best For |
|------|--------|-------------|----------|
| Co-authored blog post | Low | Indirect | Authority building |
| Joint ebook / research | Medium | Direct (gated) | Lead generation |
| Webinar | Medium | Direct | Engaged prospects |
| Product integration | High | Indirect | Long-term distribution |
| Community collaboration | Low | Indirect | Community building |

## Partnership Pitch

Don't send "we'd love to partner." Send a specific proposal:
- "We could co-author a guide on [topic] and promote to both lists. We'll write it; you review and co-brand."
- "We'd like to do a joint webinar on [topic]. We each bring 50% of the content and split promotion."

Include: proposed format, your contribution, expected audience, measurement approach.

## Agreement Structure

Document before starting:
- Lead ownership (who gets the contact data?)
- Promotion commitments (what each side will do)
- Success metrics (both parties should agree beforehand)
- Timeline and deliverables
